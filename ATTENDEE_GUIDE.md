## About the Event

HackIllinois 2026 is a **fully in-person 36-hour hackathon** running from **February 27th to March 1st**. Attendees build a software project while learning development skills from industry-led workshops and expert mentors.

---

## Before the Event

### To-dos

- **Join Discord** — RSVP'd attendees received an invite link via email. Announcements, networking, and staff Q&A all happen here.
- **Download the HackIllinois App** — Available on Apple App Store and Google Play Store. Sign in as an attendee using your **GitHub account** to streamline check-in.

### Packing List

- Student ID / Valid ID (non-UIUC students)
- Reusable water bottle (water bottles will NOT be provided)
- Laptops & chargers
- Extension cord
- Warm clothes + change of clothing
- Personal items (toiletries)
- Medication (if needed)
- Umbrella (just in case)

### Do NOT Bring

- Desktop computers
- Extra monitors
- Weapons
- Alcoholic beverages, drugs, or other illicit substances

Check-in begins at **2:00 PM on Friday, February 27th** in front of the ACM Room (1104).

## Project Requirements and Submission

### Submission

Use the **HackIllinois Devpost** to submit.

### Submission Checklist

- All team members registered, RSVP'd, and checked in.
- Submit before **Sunday, March 1st at 6:00 AM CST** (late submissions NOT accepted).
- Registered with only one team.
- Team ≤ 4 members.
- Project created during the 36-hour event.
- Project submitted only to HackIllinois 2026.
- ≥1 team member physically present at Project Showcase.
- All team members physically present for HackVoyagers Pitch (unless prior approval via email — submit [early departure form](https://go.hackillinois.org/early-departure) by end of Friday, February 27th).
- Devpost submission must contain:
  - Public GitHub repository
  - Video demo/presentation (unlisted or public, ≤3 minutes)
  - Description addressing respective track

## Judging Overview

### Track & Path Judging

- Must present at Project Showcase to be eligible for path prize.
- General Path, HackVoyagers Path, Company Tracks, and additional prizes judged via science fair-style showcase.
- **HackVoyagers Pitch** (top 7 HackVoyager teams) — Shark Tank-style pitch to judges. Room 0216, streamed.

> All group members must be HackVoyagers for the project to qualify for HackVoyagers Pitch.

### Sunday Schedule

| Time | Event |
|---|---|
| 6:00 AM | Devpost Submission deadline — check email/Discord for room allocation |
| 9:00 AM – 11:30 AM | Devpost Track Judging (General + HackVoyagers) — Project Showcase |
| 12:00 PM – 1:30 PM | HackVoyagers Pitch (Sponsored by Fulcrum) & Project Showcase Continued |
| 2:00 PM – 3:00 PM | Closing Ceremony (Awards) |

### Judging Criteria

1. **Idea & Creativity** — Originality, novelty, "out of the box" thinking.
2. **Product Design** — Usability, user flow, visual clarity, accessibility.
3. **Execution & Technical Quality** — Reliability, technical skill, sound engineering decisions.
4. **Impact & Intent** — Clarity of purpose, real problem addressed, value to users.

---

## Project Showcase

Science-fair format. **ALL teams** (HackVoyagers and General) that submitted a project present in-person.

### Format

1. Arrive ≥15 minutes early (8:45 AM). Arriving >10 minutes late = disqualification.
2. Room assignment and team number sent via email Sunday morning.
3. 120-minute presentation window. **5-minute limit per team**:
   - 3 minutes to present (then wrap up within two sentences)
   - 2-minute Q&A from judges

---

## HackVoyagers Pitch

Top 7 HackVoyager teams pitch to a panel of judges (Shark Tank-style). Company sponsors have historically reached out to standout presenters for further investment.

- **Notification**: By 11:30 AM Sunday, March 1st via email.
- **Location**: Siebel 0216

### Format

1. Arrive ≥15 minutes early.
2. **10 minutes** total:
   - ~7 minutes to pitch (wrap up within two sentences after time)
   - 3-minute Q&A from judges
3. Presentation materials: anything projectable and shareable as a link.

## Other Policies

### AI Policy

AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, LLMs) are **allowed** but **transparency is required**.

- **Must disclose** all AI tools used.
- Include a **README section** explaining:
  - Which tools you used
  - What they generated or assisted with
- Project must NOT be a simple wrapper, reskin, or minimal modification of an existing AI tool.
- Judging focuses on **what you created**, not just the tools used.
- Failure to credit AI usage may result in disqualification and can be reported to MLH.
